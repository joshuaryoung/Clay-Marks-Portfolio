WHEN HE CROSSED THE LINE
The superstar exposed himself in front
of eyewitnesses who watched sycophants
pump foreign substances into his grunt
hole in order to performance enhance.
He entered a race whose prize was mammon,
knowing that he had no chance to win it
unless whores monitored dirty urine
by dipping their crucifixes in it.
Liberal press balked when he crossed the line,
but when he pressed the Bible to his hand
his cult gushed lust hormones, blind to his crimes
until the law crashed his victory stand.
He’ll eat beautiful cake with his gold spoon
while the platform falls on one of his goons.
TESTIMONY

“Testimony” is a contemporary drama with a lean budget. It is the second installment of a regionally themed trilogy, proselytic in an antithetical way because it lays bare the neurosis of religion and its subsequent abuses. It is in fact one individual’s testimony of a gospel that does not elevate his soul but crushes it. Ten million Mormons will be admonished by the patriarchy to boycott this movie, not because the church is misrepresented but precisely because it is represented, and they want their secrets to remain secret, hidden behind the cloak of sacredness.

ANDY FOOTHILL is just ten years old when he suggests to his Sunday school teacher in a Mormon church in Salt Lake City that Jesus is a fictional manifestation of someone’s imagination. His mother, RAELYN (40), hears about his comments later that afternoon and gives him twenty lashes with her wooden spoon. RaeLyn is an overstressed, sleep-deprived mother of ten, in the throes of chronic postpartum depression. She embraces the fatiguing chores of motherhood, not because she loves children, but because she feels tyrannical allegiance to her Savior. Much of Act One is represented in flashbacks while Andy (now ELDER FOOTHILL [19]) stands at a window in his dormitory room at the Missionary Training Center, mentally rehearsing his clandestine escape.

Act Two is set in southern California, where Andy (now 25) meets and falls in love with a fellow Mormon named Francisca Seymour (FRANKY [25]). Andy is a tall, handsome, broad- shouldered descendant of the master race. He is an enigma, because emotionally he is still a little boy. Though Andy and Franky have much in common, her orientation is different than his. She was raised in Provo, but she was born in Cape Verde to parents of mixed, undocumented heritage. Her lineage is significant because the Mormon Church is just a few years removed from amending a century-enforced doctrine of Aryan supremacy founded on Brigham Young’s “curse of Cain.” Andy falls apart when Franky relocates to attend medical school at Texas Tech University. His collapse is complete when he makes an impromptu trip to Lubbock and gets his nose bloodied by the fists of Franky’s new lover, a woman named JUDY (25).

Act Three finds Andy (now 29) back in Salt Lake, living with his widowed mother. He has shoulder length hair and a beard. He is a failure, both professionally and romantically. An Oedipal theme emerges because he replaces his prematurely deceased father (VICTOR), not as RaeLyn’s bed partner but as her emotional punching bag. Painfully, Andy recognizes in retrospect that he mimicked RaeLyn’s mistreatment of his father and that he was indeed an accomplice to his figurative murder.

Following a chaotic affair with a Florida transplant named JULIA (25), Andy decides that he would be better in intimate relationships if he had a nurturing mother that he could have sexualized. Twenty years of due diligence have brought him to an understanding of the futility of his self-perpetuating struggles. God has broken promise after promise to Andy because God is an aberration. In fact, Andy’s God is an abusive woman. Andy must redefine his world, a process which begins after Franky reconnects with him and requests his DNA so that she can have a child. In the final scene, Andy gathers all of his temple garments, tosses them into an outdoor fireplace, douses them with lighter fluid, and strikes a match.

SELF-EVIDENT
I am simply not a capitalist.
I’m positive
there is a genetic
consideration. I’m a humanist
ideologically, pathetic
though it may seem, given that in practice
I am mostly just human. Some will call
me un-American, but the fact is
the nation of my birth venerates all
constitutions equally, including
an unalienable alien
who might pronounce grapes sour after brooding
over his lack of pluck in reaching them.
Conspicuous consumption insults me,
but that does not make me your enemy.
SIMPLY FAILED
In tandem, she and I were fit to burst,
though absent convenient amenities.
Recreationally satisfied thirst
sent us back to the nineteenth century.
Had she said that she wanted to see me
naked, I wouldn’t have hesitated
to expose the fleshy utility
of this simian form god created.
But the urge to excrete in her presence
catalyzed a spore of shame in the veiled
depths of my phallic phase, and a tense
sphincter with a simple job simply failed.
Universities publish futile tomes
on the cruelty of Y chromosomes.
CHARGED WITH REBELLION
The cascade is warm, they said, but they were
propagating falsehood. Years of hearsay
had you stripped bare when you chose to enter
the stream of sadistically cold spray.
Despite repeated objections you were
held in rigid deference to your will,
smashed about and suffocated under
the force of the unrelenting spillway.
Having long since pulled yourself free, you stand
on the bank and observe while others fall
into the stream, pushed by another’s hand,
frozen in their bones, unmindful of gall.
Rope in hand, you offer liberation,
aggrieved that you are charged with rebellion.
NAUTICAL VIOLATION
The lake was calm when I boarded truth boat,
but darkness a tempest accompanied.
An illumined face placed me under oath
and cross-examined to the third degree.
The skipper coerced me into sharing
the details of my secret tendencies,
thwarting false testimony and baring
matters of personal intimacy.
Waves of inquiries had me stupefied,
rocked about without a method to flee
or find justice in the merciless tide,
left to cop a plea with Neptune. Get thee
behind me, nautical violation!
That voyage I should never have taken.
CONSERVATIVE EARTH
We are little
more than a colony
of worms
the cynic observed
chasing scents,
ingesting easy fuel, enormously
adept at generating waste, and hence,
settling in our own discharges
crawling
organisms in bloodthirsty jungles,
obedient to reflexes, mauling
each other and mating in mud puddles,
disposed to spawn progeny before we
become fuel ourselves and pass through entrails
of random ravenous adversaries,
hoarded in conservative earth’s mold cells
until moist egocentric tongues protrude,
seduced by crude reconstituted food.
FOREVER GONE
I egocentrically interpret
my bubble-like environment by means
of consciousness four billion years old, yet
one contemporaneously convened.
You’re special, say looking glass deities,
though haphazardly drawn from a bloodline,
tiktaaliks fleeing predatory seas,
propelled by arms, the prototypes of mine.
Reality shared with present and past,
merely one of an infinite number
of genetic combinations so vast,
my brief existence not spared from slumber.
Part and parcel of my self will live on,
but awareness will be forever gone.
